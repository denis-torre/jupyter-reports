Investigation of BI 2536 drug as a potential drug to attenuate diabetic nephropathy progression

Aims

This notebook contains a preliminary computational analysis aimed at investigating the biological effect of BI-2536 treatment in cell lines. BI-2536 was previously identified as a candidate small molecule which capability to reverse gene expression signatures associated to diabetic nephropathy (Lin et al., 2016). For more information, please visit the diabetic nephropathy GEN3VA report page here.

Overview

In order to investigate the biological effect of BI-2536 treatment on cell lines, and its role in reversing diabetic nephropathy gene expression signatures, the following analysis was performed:

  1. First, 24 diabetic nephropathy signatures extracted from GEO were queried against the L1000 library of small molecule perturbations using L1000CDS2. This resulted in the identification of 3 BI-2536 treatment signatures, which are collectively predicted to reverse the diabetic nephropathy signatures in 13 different matches.
  2. Second, signatures corresponding to the 13 reverse matches processed in order to identify two genesets (see below for methods details):
    • A set of 310 genes downregulated in diabetic nephropathy, but upregulated by BI-2536
    • A set of 424 genes upregulated in diabetic nephropathy, but downregulated by BI-2536
  3. Third, an enrichment analysis of these genesets was performed using Enrichr:
    • Full enrichment results for the genes upregulated by BI-2536 available here.
    • Full enrichment results for the genes downregulated by BI-2536 available here.

Results

Geneset Identification

In order to identify the two genesets used for the enrichment analysis, each of the 13 reverse matches was independently queried to identify two genesets: the set of genes which are upregulated by the drug treatment and downregulated in the disease, and vice-versa. Subsequently, a count was performed in order to calculate how many times each gene appeared in each of these genesets.
